Exercise Framework

Case Scenario 1
Hands of Time vs. The Tornado
Company

Hands of Time Watch Manufacturer

Location

Oklahoma City, OK — tornado risk area

Workforce

70 employees — 50 on-site / 20 remote (US)

◔ RPO: 1 hour  |  RTO: 4 hours
Client Profile

Watch manufacturer located in Oklahoma City, where tornadoes are known to occur without warning. They need comprehensive protection across on-site servers, virtual machines, remote endpoints, and their Microsoft 365 environment.

Protection Requirements

  • Microsoft 365 — automated backups with granular restore support
  • Server 1 (Physical): 6TB — ERP & Accounting. Backups 5am–11pm daily; cloud replication every 4 hours
  • Server 2 (Physical): VMware host housing 10 VMs — image-based backup required
  • 10 VM Workstations/Desktops: Hourly backups M–F 5am–7pm; offsite replication at 5am, 12pm & 7pm; application & service verification + screenshot enabled
  • 20 Remote Endpoints: Sales team traveling nationwide — laptop data must be protected and BMR-ready
  • Local restore capability required if the appliance is still functional after a disaster
Discussion Questions
  1. 1 Which Datto BCDR recovery topology do you activate first if a tornado destroys the server room, and why?
  2. 2 How do you design the backup schedule for the VMware environment to meet the 1-hour RPO for the 10 VMs?
  3. 3 What product protects the 20 remote sales endpoints, and what restore type gets a lost or destroyed laptop back to full operation?
  4. 4 What is your communication plan to the business owner regarding the RTO commitment?

Case Scenario 2
Visual Barbering Studios
Company

Barber Shop & Full-Service Bar

Location

Rural Pennsylvania — poor network speeds

Workforce

10 employees — no remote workers; owner has travel laptops

◔ RPO: 4 hours  |  RTO: 6 hours
Client Profile

A barber shop and bar combo in rural Pennsylvania where network speeds are unreliable. Cloud offsite replication is not feasible. Owner uses laptops for travel and potential expansion planning.

Protection Requirements

  • Google Workspace — mail and document restore coverage required
  • 1 Physical Server: SQL + MySQL, 7TB — full model recovery. Full backup once per week; incremental backups remaining days. Image + database backup required
  • 3 Workstations: Backup during office hours 8am–6pm, seven days a week — data and image protection
  • Secondary copy: Replicate to a local NAS only — no cloud replication due to network latency
  • Owner laptops: Need cloud-capable backup regardless of location — endpoint protection required
  • Local restore required if appliance is still functional
Discussion Questions
  1. 1 Which backup appliance do you recommend, and why is a local NAS the right secondary copy strategy given the network constraints?
  2. 2 Which systems require backup agents, and what specific protection does each agent provide?
  3. 3 What backup schedule would you configure for the three workstations to meet business hours and the 4-hour RPO?
  4. 4 What backup type and data reduction technology protects the SQL/MySQL server on 7TB of storage?
  5. 5 How do you configure the secondary copy job to the NAS, and why is a cold copy the right method here?
  6. 6 How do you protect the owner's travel laptops given poor on-site connectivity and no on-site infrastructure?
  7. 7 What solution protects the Google Workspace data, and what risks does it address that the appliance backup cannot?

Case Scenario 3
Hidden Tiki Paradise
Company

Family-run restaurant — major tourist destination

Location

San Juan, Puerto Rico

Workforce

5 family employees — no remote workers; owner travels

◔ RPO: 2 hours  |  RTO: 4 hours
Client Profile

A tropical restaurant and major tourist attraction in San Juan, PR. Family-run with a Hyper-V virtualization environment hosting business-critical applications. Owner uses a laptop for travel and sales outreach.

Protection Requirements

  • Microsoft 365 — top priority: full tenant coverage for mailboxes, SharePoint, and Teams
  • Hyper-V Environment:
    • 1 Physical host machine housing 2 VMs
    • VM 1: Virtual Terminal for POS transactions & table management history
    • VM 2: Administration — safety, compliance, and HR data
  • VM backups — local + cloud replication every hour from 7am–11pm, seven days a week
  • Owner laptop: Full protection — Endpoint Backup required
  • Local restore required if appliance is still functional
Discussion Questions
  1. 1 How do you configure the Datto agent on the Hyper-V host to avoid duplicate backups of the VMs and the host's data volume?
  2. 2 What replication schedule do you configure to meet the 2-hour RPO during peak operating hours (7am–11pm)?
  3. 3 If the physical Hyper-V host fails during a dinner rush, which recovery topology do you activate first and why?
  4. 4 What solution and coverage scope do you propose for the Microsoft 365 environment?

Case Scenario 4
Marie's Wonderland of Sweets
Company

Bakery — two shop locations + mobile trucks

Location

Fayetteville, NC — multiple sites

Environment

2 shops (3 servers each) + 2 Mac-based bakery trucks

◔ RPO: 1 hour  |  RTO: 3 hours
Client Profile

A bakery business with two shop locations in Fayetteville, NC, and two mobile trucks that operate at events and private parties. They need local backups with cloud replication at both locations, and Google Workspace protection for operations and event coordination.

Per Shop Location (×2)

  • POS Server: Daily operations & transactions — backup window 5pm–11pm
  • ERP Server: Recipe costing & ingredient inventory — backup window 4pm–11pm
  • Inventory Management Server: Barcoding & food safety tracking — backup window 7pm–9pm

Mobile Trucks & Cloud

  • 2 Mobile Bakery Trucks: macOS devices — must replicate to cloud from any location
  • Google Workspace: Orders, events, and team coordination — full coverage required
  • Local backup + cloud replication required at both shop locations
Discussion Questions
  1. 1 How many Datto appliances are required for this environment, and where is each deployed?
  2. 2 How do you configure backup windows for three servers at each location given their different operating hours?
  3. 3 What product and configuration protects the macOS devices on the mobile trucks regardless of event location?
  4. 4 What SaaS coverage do you configure for Google Workspace, and what specific data types are protected?
  5. 5 If Location 1's POS server fails during a peak evening shift, what is the recovery plan to meet the 3-hour RTO?
